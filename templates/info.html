Danceability

Danceability is a measure of how easy or enjoyable it is for listeners to move or dance to the music. Songs with high danceability typically have a strong and steady beat, a rhythmic structure that encourages movement, and a tempo that aligns well with common dance styles.

Energy

Energy in music refers to the intensity, power, vigor, and liveliness conveyed by a song or a musical performance. It's the sense of dynamism, excitement, and drive that listeners perceive in the music. Songs with high energy typically have fast tempos, strong beats, and lively rhythms that create a sense of urgency or power.

Tempo

Tempo in music refers to the speed or pace at which a piece of music is performed or intended to be performed. It's a fundamental element that helps define the character and feel of a song. It is typically measured in beats per minute (BPM), indicating how many beats occur within one minute of time.

Acousticness

It reflects the degree to which the music is dominated by acoustic instruments as opposed to electronic or amplified instruments. Acoustic music often has a more intimate, raw, and authentic feel compared to electronically produced music.

Liveness

Liveness in music refers to the quality of a performance that conveys a sense of immediacy, energy, and spontaneity. It's the feeling that the music is being created in the moment, often in front of an audience, rather than being recorded or reproduced.

Valence

Valence refers to the emotional quality or mood conveyed by a song or a piece of music. It reflects the positivity or negativity of the emotions expressed through the music. Songs with high valence are typically perceived as more positive, cheerful, or uplifting, evoking feelings of happiness, excitement, or contentment in listeners.
